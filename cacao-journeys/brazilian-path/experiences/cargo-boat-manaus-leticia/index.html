Cargo Boat Journey: Manaus to Leticia

Multi-Day Amazon River Voyage

About This Experience

Complete your Amazon journey with an authentic cargo boat trip upriver from Manaus to Leticia, Colombia, located at the border where Brazil, Colombia, and Peru meet. This 7-day journey on the Amazon River offers a unique perspective on river life, passing through remote communities, witnessing the vastness of the rainforest, and experiencing the rhythm of river transportation that has connected Amazonian communities for centuries.

Life on the River: Sleep in hammocks on deck, sharing the space with local passengers, cargo, and the gentle sway of the boat. This is how most Amazonian communities travel—slowly, together, with the river as both highway and home. The experience strips away modern conveniences, connecting you to the essential rhythm of river life.

Remote Communities: As the boat makes stops along the way, you'll witness the daily life of riverside communities. See how people live in harmony with the river, depending on it for transportation, food, and connection. These brief encounters offer glimpses into a way of life that has persisted for generations.

The Vastness of the Amazon: Watch the ever-changing landscape of the world's greatest river unfold before you. The scale is humbling—miles of uninterrupted rainforest, tributaries branching in every direction, and the sense that you're traveling through one of the planet's last great wildernesses.

Reflection and Connection: This slow journey allows for deep reflection on the interconnectedness of the Amazon ecosystem. As you travel upriver, consider how the same waters that flow past regenerative cacao farms also flow past communities, cities, and eventually to the ocean. The river connects everything, just as regenerative agriculture connects land, people, and purpose.

Arrival in Leticia: Your journey concludes in Leticia, Colombia, where the borders of Brazil, Colombia, and Peru meet. This tri-border region represents the international nature of the Amazon—a shared resource that requires cooperation and care across nations.

This cargo boat journey is more than transportation—it's a meditation on time, space, and the essential connections that bind us to the natural world. It's a fitting conclusion to a journey that began with innovation in Florianópolis, passed through regenerative farms in Bahia, immersed you in culture, and brought you deep into the heart of the Amazon.
