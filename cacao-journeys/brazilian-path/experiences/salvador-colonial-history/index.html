Salvador: Portuguese Colonial History

Brazil's First Capital & UNESCO World Heritage Site

About This Experience

Explore Salvador, the first capital of Brazil and a UNESCO World Heritage site. This vibrant city offers a profound journey through Brazil's complex history, where Portuguese colonial architecture meets the rich Afro-Brazilian culture that defines Bahia.

Pelourinho Historic Center: Wander through Pelourinho, the historic heart of Salvador, where colorful colonial buildings line cobblestone streets. This neighborhood tells the story of Brazil's colonial past, from the Portuguese arrival to the complex legacy of the transatlantic slave trade. The architecture itself is a testament to the wealth generated by sugar and cacao plantations, while also bearing witness to the resilience of African culture in Brazil.

São Francisco Church: Visit one of Brazil's most stunning baroque churches, with its gold-covered interior that reflects the opulence of colonial Brazil. The intricate carvings and gilded decorations create an atmosphere of awe, while also prompting reflection on the labor and resources that made such beauty possible.

Afro-Brazilian Culture: Salvador is the center of Afro-Brazilian culture in Brazil, with over 80% of its population having African ancestry. Experience the vibrant music, dance, and religious traditions—from Candomblé ceremonies to the rhythms of Olodum—that have shaped Brazilian identity. The city's cuisine, with its African influences and use of dendê oil, offers another dimension of this cultural fusion.

Historical Reflection: Before continuing your journey north to the Amazon, take time in Salvador to reflect on the interconnected histories of colonization, agriculture, and cultural resistance. The same forces that shaped Salvador's history also shaped the cacao industry—understanding this context deepens your appreciation for regenerative practices that honor both land and people.

Salvador serves as a crucial waypoint in the Brazilian journey, offering historical context and cultural depth before you fly to Manaus to begin your Amazon adventures.
