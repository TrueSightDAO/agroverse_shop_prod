Agroverse Cacao Journeys

Journeys connecting communities, farms, and the regenerative journey of cacao. From self-guided seasonal migrations along the Pacific West Coast to transformative journeys through Brazil's regenerative farms, these journeys invite you to wander with intention, connecting with the land, the people, and the transformative power of regenerative cacao.

Each journey offers a unique path of discovery. The Pacific West Coast Journey is a self-guided, seasonal migration from Spring to Winter, connecting partner venues and experiences where communities gather around regenerative cacao. The Brazilian Journey takes you through regenerative cacao farms, cultural experiences, and Amazon adventures. Both journeys invite mindful connection and conscious transformation.

The Pacific West Coast Journey

A self-guided journey from Washington's borderlands near Canada to the California and Arizona desert, connecting communities through regenerative cacao. Beginning in Spring (May) with the Okanogan Family Barter Faire and culminating in Winter (Thanksgiving period) with desert gatherings, this seasonal migration follows a path reminiscent of the Pacific Crest Trail. Experience diverse spaces—from mountain gatherings to urban sanctuaries, from the off-grid community of Slab City & Salvation Mountain to the vibrant Winter Desert Gatherings in Quartzite—where regenerative cacao creates spaces for connection, celebration, and transformation.

The Brazilian Drift

Embark on a transformative journey through Brazil, connecting regenerative cacao farms, cultural experiences, and Amazon adventures. Starting from Founderhaus in Florianópolis, travel north through the farms of Bahia, immerse yourself in capoeira and samba in Itacaré, explore Portuguese colonial history in Salvador, then journey deep into the Amazon for jungle adventures and river voyages to Leticia.
