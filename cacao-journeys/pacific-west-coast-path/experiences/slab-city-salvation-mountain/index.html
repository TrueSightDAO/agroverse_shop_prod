Slab City & Salvation Mountain

The Last Free Place in America

About This Experience

Nestled in the California desert near the Salton Sea, Slab City stands as one of America's most unique communities—the "Last Free Place in America." This off-grid settlement, built on the concrete slabs of a former military base, has become a haven for free spirits, artists, nomads, and those seeking an alternative way of life beyond the constraints of modern society.

Slab City: A community without government, utilities, or traditional infrastructure, Slab City operates on principles of mutual aid, freedom, and self-reliance. Here, residents and visitors create their own rules, generate their own power, and build community through shared values and respect. The community is known for its vibrant art installations, music events, and gatherings that celebrate freedom and creative expression.

Salvation Mountain: Just outside Slab City stands Salvation Mountain, a massive, colorful art installation created by Leonard Knight over three decades. This vibrant monument, made from adobe, straw, and thousands of gallons of paint, stands as a testament to faith, love, and artistic vision. The mountain features biblical messages, colorful designs, and has been recognized as a national treasure, drawing visitors from around the world.

The Ponderosa: Within this unique desert landscape, The Ponderosa offers a welcoming space for visitors to experience Slab City and Salvation Mountain. Run by Rodney "Spyder" and Shannon, this homesteading haven combines rugged desert living with a vision for sustainability, featuring solar power, livestock, and plans for aquaponics. As an Agroverse partner venue, The Ponderosa incorporates regenerative ceremonial cacao into gatherings, creating spaces for connection, reflection, and celebration in the heart of this free-spirited community.

This experience represents a powerful intersection of freedom, art, community, and conscious living—a place where regenerative cacao serves as a bridge between individuals and the larger community, supporting both local connection and rainforest restoration.
