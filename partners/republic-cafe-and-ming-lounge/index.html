About Republic Cafe & Ming Lounge

Nestled at 222 NW 4th Ave, Portland, OR 97209, Republic Cafe & Ming Lounge stands as a cornerstone of Portland's Old Town-Chinatown, proudly operating as one of the city's oldest continuously running Chinese restaurants since its establishment in 1922. Owned and operated by the Mui family for over three decades, this historic venue is housed in the O.B. Stubbs Building, part of the Portland New Chinatown–Japantown Historic District, listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1989.

The Republic Cafe is renowned for its classic Cantonese dishes, such as Mongolian beef, chop suey, and egg foo young, served in generous portions at affordable prices. Its enduring appeal lies not only in its food but in its role as a cultural and social hub, historically attracting a diverse clientele—from local tastemakers to celebrities in its 1950s heyday when it stayed open until 4 a.m. on weekends.

The adjacent Ming Lounge, one of Portland's oldest bars, exudes a nostalgic, dive-bar charm with its red and gold oriental decor, hosting everything from live music and comedy nights to maker fairs and art shows in its repurposed banquet and gallery spaces. Despite its "seedy" reputation and dated interior, the venue's unpolished authenticity is part of its allure, offering a warm, welcoming space where community thrives.

Republic Cafe & Ming Lounge continues to evolve, embracing modern events while preserving its rich history, making it an ideal partner for Agroverse.shop to share our ethically sourced cacao with Portland's vibrant community.
